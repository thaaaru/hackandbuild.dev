Interactive DIKW Hierarchy Explorer

Explore the Data-Information-Knowledge-Wisdom hierarchy with practical examples and interactive elements.

Data

Definition: Raw facts and figures without context.

Example: Temperature readings: 25°C, 30°C, 28°C, 32°C

Characteristics: Unprocessed, objective, lacks meaning

Information

Definition: Processed data that has been given context and meaning.

Example: "The average temperature this week was 28.75°C, which is 3°C above normal."

Characteristics: Processed, contextual, answers "what" and "when"

Knowledge

Definition: Information combined with experience, skills, and understanding.

Example: "Based on historical patterns, temperatures above 30°C typically increase energy consumption by 15% due to air conditioning usage."

Characteristics: Experiential, actionable, answers "how" and "why"

Wisdom

Definition: Knowledge applied with good judgment and ethical considerations.

Example: "We should implement energy-efficient cooling strategies and educate residents about conservation to balance comfort with environmental responsibility."

Characteristics: Judgmental, ethical, answers "should we" and considers long-term impact
